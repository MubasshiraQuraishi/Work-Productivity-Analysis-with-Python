## üîç Project Overview

This project explores how **work-from-home (WFH) practices, managerial support, infrastructure quality, collaboration, and experience** influence:

* **Employee Productivity**
* **Operational Efficiency**
* **Employee Well-Being & Job Satisfaction**

Using a real-world structured dataset, the analysis combines **exploratory data analysis (EDA), visualization, and hypothesis testing** to uncover actionable business insights that can guide **modern workforce strategy and organizational decision-making**.

---

## üéØ Objectives

* Understand how **WFH intensity impacts productivity and efficiency**
* Identify the **optimal hybrid work model**
* Evaluate the impact of **home-office quality and internet infrastructure**
* Measure how **managerial support affects stress and satisfaction**
* Analyze the relationship between **experience and performance**
* Study how **collaboration frequency and meeting load influence outcomes**
* Provide **business-driven recommendations** rather than only statistical results

## üìå Key Business Insights

* **Hybrid work (4 days WFH) delivers peak productivity and efficiency**, outperforming fully remote and mostly in-office models.
* **Good and average home-office setups outperform extreme setups**, indicating diminishing returns beyond functional comfort.
* **Manager support significantly improves job satisfaction**, but does not directly reduce stress ‚Äî workload design plays a stronger role.
* **7‚Äì8 meetings per week produce optimal productivity**, balancing coordination and deep work.
* **Daily collaboration drives the highest innovation**, though satisfaction remains unaffected.
* **Experience boosts performance up to mid-career**, followed by a notable dip ‚Äî suggesting burnout or role stagnation.
* **Commute time no longer strongly impacts work-life balance**, reinforcing the value of hybrid work models.
